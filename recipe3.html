Chole Bhature

Prep Time: 30 minutes + soaking
Cook Time: 45 minutes
Servings: 4
Category: Vegetarian

Ingredients

For Chole:

  • 2 cups kala chana (black chickpeas), soaked overnight
  • 3 cups water (for cooking chana)
  • 3 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon dry mango powder (amchur)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

For Bhature:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Water as needed
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions

  • Drain and rinse soaked chana; pressure cook with salted water until soft.
  • Sauté cumin seeds, onions, ginger-garlic paste, and tomato puree.
  • Add spices, then chana; simmer until thick and flavorful.
  • Finish with amchur, garam masala, and coriander.
  • For bhature: make dough, rest 1 hour, roll discs, and deep fry.
  • Serve chole with bhature, onion, lemon, and pickle.

Chef's Tips

Pro Tip 1: Use fresh chana and properly pre-soak overnight so they cook soft and absorb spices well.

Pro Tip 2: Add a small piece of jaggery or sugar to the chole for balanced sweetness with tanginess.

Pro Tip 3: Ensure the oil is properly hot for bhature so they puff well without absorbing excess oil.
